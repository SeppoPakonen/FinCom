# Capital Loan Bond (Template)

This is a template for a capital loan bond. A capital loan is a loan whose repayment and interest are restricted in accordance with the provisions of the Limited Liability Companies Act.

### 2. Repayment of the Loan
The principal and interest of the loan may only be repaid with a lower priority than all other creditors in the company's liquidation or bankruptcy. Otherwise, the principal may be repaid and interest paid only to the extent that the total amount of the company's free equity and all capital loans exceeds the amount of losses shown in the company's most recently approved balance sheet or a more recent balance sheet included in the financial statements at the time of payment.

If the above conditions do not restrict repayment, the capital loan shall be repaid:
- **Amortization:**       euros on the last day of each month.
- **Start date:**   .  .202

### 3. Interest
- Annual interest is the Government Finance Ministry's base rate plus       percentage points.
- If interest cannot be paid (see Section 2), it will be payable based on the first subsequent financial statement that allows payment.
- Interest accrued on the loan but not yet expensed is reported annually in the financial statement's supplementary information.

### 4. Collateral
The company or its subsidiary does not provide collateral for the payment of the loan principal or interest.
