# PRH: Minor in a Limited Liability Company

A person under 18 years of age (minor) can serve as a share subscriber and sign the articles of incorporation of a limited liability company in certain situations.

## Situations where a minor can subscribe to shares
1. **Own earnings:** A minor can subscribe to shares themselves if they pay for them with their own earnings.
2. **Gift situation:** Guardians subscribe to shares on behalf of the minor (if not using the minor's own earnings).
3. **Guardian subscribing with minor's assets:** Requires permission from the Digital and Population Data Services Agency (DVV).
4. **Conflict of interest situation:** If the guardian is themselves a party to the articles of incorporation or a board member, a substitute must be requested from DVV for the subscription.
5. **Management of apartments:** If the shares entitle to management of apartments, DVV permission is required.

## Limitations
* A minor **cannot** serve as a board member or managing director of a limited liability company.
