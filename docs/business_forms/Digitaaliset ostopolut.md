# Digital Shopping Paths and E-commerce Planning

This document covers digital business operations, success factors for e-commerce, and strategic marketing planning from the customer's purchase journey perspective.

---

## 1. Digital Business and Commerce Transformation

Digital commerce encompasses all sales-oriented activities in a digital environment.
- **Objectives:** Increase revenue, improve profitability and enhance efficiency.
- **Transformation:** Previously, customers chose the store; now they choose the product and purchase it from the most convenient channel for them. Power has shifted to the customer.
- **Direct-to-consumer:** Brands and manufacturers increasingly sell directly to consumers (D2C), bypassing traditional retailers.

---

## 2. Success Factors for E-commerce

Successful e-commerce is built on three pillars:
1. **Genuine customer understanding:** Why and which customers buy from us?
2. **Customer promise:** Meaningfulness, distinctiveness and credibility.
3. **Strategic packaging:** Customer-oriented productization of offerings in digital format.

### Strategic choices:
- **Channel strategy:** Factors affecting the choice include profitability, customer ownership, customer acquisition costs and brand control.
- **Product strategy:** Assortment breadth, category structure and operational capability (logistics, scalability).
- **Content strategy:** Good content is an essential competitive tool. It is built on customer knowledge and community.

---

## 3. Establishing and Developing E-commerce

### Planning phases:
- **Setting objectives:** Serving existing customers vs. international scaling.
- **Platform selection:** (E.g. WooCommerce, Shopify, MyCashFlow, Vilkas, Finqu).
- **Booking systems:** (E.g. Timma, Vello, DigitalBooker).
- **Practical implementation:** Appearance, structure, payment methods, logistics and testing on different devices.

---

## 4. Digital Purchase Journey and REAN Model

Understanding the purchase journey is part of quality customer service. Customer service has four roles along the purchase journey: **Trust, Expertise, Service and Sales**.

### REAN model along the purchase journey:

| Phase | Objective | Actions and metrics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **REACH** | Be discovered. | Content marketing, SEO/SEM, social media, influencers. (Metric: visitors, followers). |
| **ENGAGE** | Activate and convince. | Engaging content, automated reminders. (Metric: likes, shares, conversions). |
| **ACTIVATE** | Enable action. | Conversion optimization, clear purchase process. (Metric: number of purchases, average order value). |
| **NURTURE** | Encourage return. | After-sales marketing, email, social media. (Metric: newsletter subscribers, mentions). |

## 6. Social Media Publishing Calendar

Planning ensures goal-oriented communication:
- **Daily:** Check channels and respond.
- **Weekly:** Publish regular content.
- **Monthly:** Major theme, campaign or blog post.

---

## 7. E-commerce responsibilities and documents

E-commerce operators must comply with consumer protection and data protection laws:
- **Contact information:** Company name, location and clear contact details.
- **Terms of delivery:** Payment methods, delivery times and costs.
- **Return policy:** General 14-day right of withdrawal. For customized products, it must be stated separately if they cannot be returned.
- **Product information:** Key features and total price including tax.
- **Strong authentication:** Rules according to the Payment Services Directive (PSD2).

---

## 8. Future challenges
- **Individualization:** Moving from mass service to individual experiences.
- **Algorithms:** Standing out and discoverability in search engines/social media becomes more difficult.
- **Simplicity:** Technological complexity must not spoil the user experience.
- **Environment:** Sustainability issues in logistics and consumption culture.
