# Tool: Occupational Safety Action Plan

This document defines the company's general objectives and practices for occupational safety. Occupational safety refers to cooperation between the employer and employees to promote health and safety.

---

## 1. Occupational Safety Objectives
* Prevent workplace accidents and occupational diseases.
* Maintain and promote employees' work ability and well-being.
* Ensure that each employee knows safe working methods.

## 2. Responsibilities and Authority
* **Employer/Supervisors:** Responsible for ensuring the work environment is safe and employees are oriented.
* **Employees:** Obligation to follow instructions and report any deficiencies or hazards noticed.

## 3. Work Environment Description and Risk Assessment
* Description of workspaces and tasks.
* Risk assessments are conducted regularly (or during change phases).
* Identified hazards and their management methods are recorded in a separate risk assessment.

## 4. Work and Safety Instructions
* **Orientation:** Each new employee is oriented in safe working methods.
* **First Aid:** The workplace has adequate first aid supplies and responsible persons are designated.
* **Emergency Situations:** Instructions for threats of violence, accidents or fires.

## 5. Development Targets
* List items that need improvement (e.g. lighting, ergonomics, updating instructions).

* **Contract:** [Occupational health care contract date]
* **Action Plan:** Updated annually based on workplace survey.

## 7. Monitoring and Updates
* Working conditions are monitored using accident statistics, sick leave records and workplace atmosphere surveys.
* The action plan is reviewed and updated at least once a year.
