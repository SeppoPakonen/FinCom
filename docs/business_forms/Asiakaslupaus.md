# Customer Promise: Principles and Examples

This document defines the significance of a customer promise, its foundations and provides examples of how to formulate different types of promises.

---

## 1. Foundations of a Customer Promise

A customer promise is a central part of a company's marketing and brand:
- **Message carrier:** Highlights the value of the company's brand and product or service to the customer.
- **Differentiator:** Illustrates why the company is better than its competitors.
- **Commitment:** Assurance of what customers can expect from products, services and the overall customer experience.
- **Guideline:** Helps the company focus on specific values and principles of operation.

A well-formulated customer promise creates a stronger brand, increases customer loyalty and attracts new customers.

---

## 2. Examples of Customer Promises

### Quality and Reliability
> "We offer high-quality products and services that you can trust to meet your needs and expectations."

### Customer Focus
> "Our customers are our top priority. We listen to your needs and strive to provide customized solutions."

### Innovation and Latest Solutions
> "We are pioneers in our field and continuously bring new and innovative solutions to the market."

### Environmental Responsibility
> "We are committed to sustainable operations and strive to minimize our environmental impact in everything we do."

### Convenience and Comfort
> "Our goal is to make your business with us as effortless and pleasant as possible in every encounter."

### Competitive Prices
> "We offer quality products at competitive prices, so you get the best value for your money."

### Expertise and Support
> "Our team's expertise and support are always available to ensure you achieve your goals."

---

## 3. Implementation Considerations

- **Honesty:** It is critical that the customer promise is honest and realistically achievable.
- **Guiding operations:** The promise should guide all operations of the company so that it reflects in every customer encounter.
- **Adaptability:** A customer promise may change over time to respond to market changes and new customer needs.
