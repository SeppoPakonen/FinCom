# Examples of Limited Liability Company Articles of Association

This page contains 2 alternative examples of articles of association for a limited liability company. Use either of these examples as a model for your company's articles of association.

## Limited Liability Company Articles of Association – Example B

Section 1 The company's trade name is

Section 2 The company's registered office is

Section 3 The company's business activity is

Section 4 The company has a board consisting of one to five regular members and at least one alternate member if fewer than three regular members are elected to the board. The term of office of the board members continues until further notice.

Section 5 The company is represented by the board, as well as by the chairperson of the board and the managing director, each individually, and by two board members together. In addition, the board may grant procuration or the right to represent the company to a named person.

Section 6 The notice of a general meeting must be delivered at least two months but no later than one week before the meeting in writing to each shareholder whose address is known to the company.

Section 7 The annual general meeting must be held annually on a date determined by the board within six months of the end of the financial year.

At the annual general meeting, decisions must be made on:
- confirmation of the financial statements
- appropriation of the profit shown in the balance sheet
- discharge from liability for the members of the board and the managing director
- if necessary, election of the board and auditor

Section 8 The shareholder and the company have the right to redeem shares transferring from other than the company to another party.

The shareholder has the primary right of redemption. If several shareholders wish to exercise their right of redemption, the shares are divided among those wishing to redeem in proportion to the shares they own. If the division of shares does not divide evenly, the remaining shares are divided among those wishing to redeem by lot. The company has the right of redemption if none of the shareholders redeems the shares being transferred.

Otherwise, the provisions of the Limited Liability Companies Act apply to redemption.
