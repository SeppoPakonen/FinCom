# Supervisor Induction: Key Legislation

This document compiles critical employment legislation for supervisors, employer obligations and principles of managing workplace well-being.

---

## 1. Supervisor Competence Framework

The supervisor's primary task is to implement the company's strategy by guiding their subordinates. Competence consists of three areas:
- **Motivation:** Willingness to act as a supervisor, self-awareness and desire to develop.
- **Skills:** Communication and interaction skills, emotional intelligence and use of management tools.
- **Knowledge:** Strategic objectives, understanding of role and responsibilities, and legal matters.

The supervisor acts as the employer's representative and is responsible for the unit's operations, finances and supporting employees' professional development.

---

## 2. Managing Workplace Well-being

Workplace well-being is safe, healthy and productive work in a well-managed organization.
- **Development targets:** Individual health, team functionality, work environment and professional competence.
- **Ethical management:** Value-based actions that commit to improving productivity and well-being simultaneously.
- **Metrics:** Sickness absences, accident frequency, staff turnover, quality of development discussions and workplace climate surveys.

---

## 3. Induction and On-the-job Guidance

Induction is the employer's responsibility. Its purpose is to create positive attitudes, promote independence and ensure occupational health and safety.
- **Contents:** Organization's practices, terms of employment contract, working hours, salary matters, occupational health care, safety issues and personal responsibility areas.

---

## 4. Key Employment Legislation

### Employment Contracts Act (55/2001)
Creates the foundation for employment relationship matters.
- **Employer's obligations:** Equal treatment, non-discrimination, ensuring workplace safety and written explanation of work conditions.
- **Probationary period:** Maximum 6 months.

### Occupational Safety and Health Act (738/2002)
The basic law of occupational protection.
- **Employer's obligation:** Ensure employees' safety and health, identify hazards and assess risks.
- **Employee's obligation:** Follow instructions, use protective equipment and report defects.

### Working Hours Act (872/2019)
- **Regular working hours:** 8 h/day and 40 h/week. Possibility for flexible working hours and time banking.
- **Rest periods:** Generally at least 11 h daily rest and 35 h weekly rest.

### Other key laws:
- **Equality Act:** Promotes equality between women and men (equality plan mandatory if at least 30 employees).
- **Non-Discrimination Act:** Prevents discrimination (age, origin, religion, etc.).
- **Act on Privacy Protection in Working Life:** Processing of personal data and camera surveillance.
- **Occupational Health Care Act:** Obliges arranging services to prevent diseases.
- **Annual Holidays Act:** Defines holiday length, pay and timing.

---

## 5. Collective Agreements (TES/VES)

- **General applicability:** TES applies also to non-organized employers throughout the industry.
- **Minimum requirements:** TES defines minimum wages and other conditions that must not be exceeded. Better conditions can be agreed locally.
