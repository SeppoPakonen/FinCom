# ABC of Working Life Rules and Obligations

## Overview
This document provides a comprehensive overview of the fundamental rules and obligations in Finnish working life. It covers employment legislation, workplace safety requirements, and the rights and responsibilities of both employers and employees.

## Key Employment Legislation

### 1. Employment Contracts Act
- All employment relationships must be governed by a written contract if lasting more than 2 months
- Contract must specify job duties, salary, working hours, and vacation entitlement
- Changes to contract terms require mutual agreement

### 2. Working Hours Act
- Standard working day is 8 hours
- Average working time cannot exceed 40 hours per week over a 4-month period
- Overtime must be compensated with premium pay or time off
- Minimum daily rest period is 11 consecutive hours

### 3. Annual Holidays Act
- Employees earn 2.5 vacation days per month worked
- Minimum annual vacation is 24 working days
- Vacation must be taken within the holiday year (April-March)
- Employers and employees must agree on vacation scheduling

### 4. Occupational Safety and Health Act
- Employers must provide a safe working environment
- Risk assessments must be conducted annually
- Employees must complete safety training before starting work
- Incident reporting is mandatory for employers

## Rights and Responsibilities

### Employee Rights
- Right to fair compensation
- Right to a safe workplace
- Right to vacation and leave
- Right to non-discrimination
- Right to professional development opportunities

### Employee Obligations
- Duty to perform assigned work tasks
- Duty to follow company policies
- Duty to maintain confidentiality
- Duty to report workplace hazards
- Duty to participate in required training

### Employer Rights
- Right to expect work performance
- Right to manage business operations
- Right to make operational decisions
- Right to enforce company policies

### Employer Obligations
- Provide safe working conditions
- Pay agreed salary and benefits
- Follow employment legislation
- Respect employee rights
- Maintain required insurance coverage

## Workplace Safety Requirements

### 1. Risk Assessment
- Conduct annual risk assessments
- Update assessments when conditions change
- Document all identified risks
- Implement mitigation measures

### 2. Safety Equipment
- Provide necessary safety equipment at no cost to employees
- Ensure equipment meets safety standards
- Maintain equipment in good condition
- Train employees on proper use

### 3. Training Requirements
- All employees must complete safety training before starting work
- Regular refresher training required
- Specialized training for hazardous tasks
- Documentation of all training completed

## Compensation and Benefits

### 1. Salary Payment
- Salaries must be paid at least monthly
- Payment must be made within 10 days of period end
- Overtime compensation required
- Holiday pay calculated separately

### 2. Social Security Contributions
- Employers must contribute to social security
- Contributions include pension, unemployment, and health insurance
- Rates vary based on employee category
- Payments due monthly

### 3. Additional Benefits
- Possible additional benefits through collective agreements
- Company-specific benefits may be offered
- Benefits must be documented in employment contract
- Equal treatment required for all employees

## Termination Procedures

### 1. Notice Periods
- Notice periods vary based on length of employment
- Minimum notice periods defined by law
- Longer periods may be agreed contractually
- Collective agreements may specify additional requirements

### 2. Grounds for Termination
- Valid business reasons required
- Following proper procedures mandatory
- Opportunity to respond to allegations
- Non-discriminatory basis required

### 3. Severance Pay
- May be required in certain situations
- Amount based on length of employment
- Collective agreements may specify additional requirements
- Calculated according to statutory formulas

## Compliance and Monitoring

### 1. Labor Inspectorate
- Government body overseeing employment compliance
- Conducts inspections of workplaces
- Enforces employment legislation
- Provides guidance to employers and employees

### 2. Documentation Requirements
- Maintain employment records
- Document safety measures and training
- Keep records of working hours
- Preserve employee communications

### 3. Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Financial penalties for violations
- Possible criminal charges for serious violations
- Remedial orders to correct violations
- Public disclosure of violations
