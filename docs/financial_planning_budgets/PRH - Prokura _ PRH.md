# PRH: Procuration (Attorneys)

A procuration is a broad commercial authorization that allows the designated person (attorney) to represent the company in all business matters.

## 1. Attorney's powers
* **Scope:** May act on behalf of the principal and sign the business name in matters related to business operations.
* **Limitations:** Without special authorization, may not transfer real estate or leasehold rights or apply for mortgages to them.
* **Legal representation:** Written procuration entitles to represent the company in courts.
* **Note:** An attorney **cannot** sign Trade Register notifications on behalf of the company.

## 2. Notification and fee
* **Voluntary:** Notifying new attorneys is voluntary, but recommended.
* **Changes:** Changes to already registered information must be reported without delay.

## 3. Revocation
* A procuration can be revoked at any time by the granter (board or partners).
* Revocation must always be reported to the Trade Register so that it is publicly known.
