# PRH: European Economic Area (EEA) and Business Operations

If a person does not have a permanent residence in the EEA area, they may need PRH's permission to perform responsible or management duties in a company in Finland.

## 1. Which countries belong to the EEA area?
The EEA area includes:
* **EU countries:** Netherlands, Belgium, Bulgaria, Spain, Ireland, Italy, Austria, Greece, Croatia, Cyprus, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Portugal, Poland, France, Romania, Sweden, Germany, Slovakia, Slovenia, Finland, Denmark, Czech Republic, Hungary, Estonia.
* **Others:** Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway.
* **Note:** Great Britain does not belong to the EEA area (left the EU on 1.1.2021).

## 2. When do you need a permit?
A permit is required if a person belonging to management permanently resides outside the EEA area. The obligation concerns, among others:
* Private entrepreneurs.
* Members of the board of directors and managing director of a limited liability company (depending on whether at least one member resides in the EEA area).
* Partners of an open company.

## 3. Applying for a Permit
A permit is applied for from PRH during the establishment of the company or during a change of responsible personnel. Each business form has its own instructions and forms for applying for a permit.
