# Examples of Articles of Association for Limited Liability Companies

This page contains 2 alternative examples of articles of association for limited liability companies. Use either of these examples as a model for your company's articles of association.

Copy the sample text to a word processing program and add the company's information to the text. Modify and add provisions as needed.

## Articles of Association for Limited Liability Company – Example B

1 § The company's trade name is

2 § The company's domicile is

3 § The company's business is

4 § The company has a board of directors consisting of one to five regular members and at least one alternate member if fewer than three regular members are elected to the board of directors. The term of office of the board members continues indefinitely.

5 § The company is represented by the board of directors, as well as by the chairperson of the board of directors and the managing director, each individually, and by two board members together. In addition, the board of directors can grant a named person a power of attorney or right to represent the company.

6 § Notice of a general meeting must be delivered at the earliest two months and at the latest one week before the general meeting in writing to each shareholder whose address is known to the company.

7 § An ordinary general meeting must be held annually on a date determined by the board of directors within six months of the end of the financial period.

At the ordinary general meeting, decisions must be made on:

approval of the financial statements
use of the profit shown in the balance sheet
exemption from liability for members of the board of directors and the managing director
election of the board of directors and auditor when necessary.

8 § The shareholder and the company have the right to redeem shares transferred from another owner than the company to another party.

The shareholder has the primary right of redemption. If several shareholders wish to exercise the right of redemption, the shares are divided among those wishing to redeem them in proportion to the shares they own. If the division of shares does not divide evenly, the remaining shares are allocated among those wishing to redeem them by lot. The company has the right of redemption if none of the shareholders redeems the shares being transferred.

Otherwise, the provisions of the Limited Liability Companies Act apply to redemption.
