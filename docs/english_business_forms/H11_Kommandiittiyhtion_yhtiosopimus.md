# Limited Partnership Agreement (Templates)

This document contains templates for establishing a limited partnership (Kommandiittiyhtiö, Ky). A limited partnership must have at least one **general partner** and one **silent partner**.

---

## Filling Instructions and Notes
- Fill in the required fields (business name, registered office, industry, contributions).
- Remember to sign after printing.
- **Shareholder/Partner Agreement:** Recommended if there is more than one general partner.
- **Privacy:** Personal information (identity code, home address) is reported separately to the Trade Register.
- **Disclaimer:** These templates are examples; the creator is not responsible for their suitability in individual cases.

### 4. Managing director and representation
- A managing director can be appointed for ongoing administration.
- The general partner represents the company.

### 5. Profit sharing
- The silent partner receives an agreed annual interest on the capital contribution from the profits (payment by end of January).
- If profits are insufficient, the shortfall is paid from the following year's profits.
- Any excess over the profit share goes to the general partner.

### 6. Partner's death and transfer of share
- The company does not dissolve upon death; legal heirs take the place of the deceased (requires 18 years of age).
- Redemption price: Company's assets - liabilities, reduced by 30 percent.
- **Transfer right:** The silent partner may not transfer their share without the consent of the general partner.

### 7. Dissolution
- Notice period: 6 months.
- Upon dissolution, after debts are paid, the silent partner's contribution and interest are paid first; the remainder goes to the general partner.

## TEMPLATE 2: Two general partners and one silent partner

*(Same as Template 1, with the following additions)*

- **Relationship between general partners:** Both general partners have the right to represent the company and manage its affairs unless otherwise agreed.
- **Profit sharing:** After the silent partner's interest, the remaining profit is shared between the general partners according to their contributions or by separate agreement.
- **Dissolution:** If one general partner terminates the agreement, the other may continue operations by redeeming the departing party's share.
