# Summary: Occupational Safety Act (738/2002)

The purpose of the law is to improve the work environment and working conditions, prevent workplace accidents and occupational diseases, and ensure employees' work capacity.

## 1. Employer's General Duty of Care
* The employer is obligated to ensure the safety and health of employees in all areas related to work.
* Considerations: working conditions, the employee's personal prerequisites (e.g., age, professional skills), and technological advancement.

## 2. Identifying and Assessing Hazards (Risk Assessment)
* The employer must identify harmful and hazardous factors arising from work.
* If a hazard cannot be eliminated, its significance must be assessed and precautions taken (e.g., protective equipment).
* Special attention must be paid to: physical and mental stress, threat of violence, working alone, and night work.

## 3. Instruction and Guidance
* The employer must provide the employee with sufficient information about workplace hazards.
* The employee must be oriented on the work, work tools, and safe working methods before starting work.

## 4. Employee Obligations
* Follow the safety instructions and orders given by the employer.
* Ensure the safety of themselves and others using available means.
* Use and maintain the personal protective equipment provided by the employer.
* **Reporting obligation:** Immediately report defects and deficiencies that may cause danger.

## 5. Right to Refuse Work
* The employee has the right to refrain from work if it poses serious danger to life or health. This must be reported to the employer immediately.

## 6. Workplace Structures and Work Tools
* Workstations must be designed ergonomically (e.g., display work).
* Machines and equipment must be safe and regularly maintained.
* The workplace must have a sufficient number of first aid supplies and instructions for their use.

---
**Remember:** Occupational safety is based on continuous monitoring and cooperation between the employer and employees.
