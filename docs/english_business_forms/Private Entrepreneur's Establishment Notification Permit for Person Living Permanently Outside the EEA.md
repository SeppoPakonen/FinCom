# Private Entrepreneur's Establishment Notification - Permit for Person Living Permanently Outside the EEA

If a person does not have a permanent residence in the European Economic Area (EEA), they need a permit from PRH to operate as a private entrepreneur (trading as a business) in Finland.

PRH has practically granted permits to people who permanently reside in Switzerland. This practice is based on the Lugano Convention. PRH evaluates and decides on the permit application only during processing.

The person's citizenship does not determine the need for a permit.

When do you apply for a permit?

You must first obtain a permit from PRH before you can make a registration notification for a private entrepreneur (trading as a business).

## What attachments do you need?

Attach to the application a copy of the applicant's passport or other identity document if the applicant does not have a Finnish personal identity code.

## How do you apply for a permit?

You can apply for a permit from PRH either by using a form or with a free-form written application.

### How do you apply for a permit using a form?

Fill in the permit application form for a person living permanently outside the EEA who intends to operate as a private entrepreneur in Finland. Send the dated and signed form with attachments to PRH.

Also state in the application the name of a possible legal representative or other contact person. Report the contact person's contact information on the personal information form.

[Open: Personal Information Form](https://www.prh.fi/material/sites/prh/forms/kaupparekisteri_fi/XeyHsmAuI/henkilotietolomake.pdf).

### How do you apply for a permit with a free-form written application?

You can also apply for a permit with a free-form written application in Finnish or Swedish.

State the following in the application:

* Applicant's name, date of birth, citizenship, place of residence (municipality or city) and the country where the place of residence is located.
* Company name and business ID, if the applicant already has one.
* Apply for a permit to operate as a private entrepreneur in Finland.

Also state in the application the name of a possible legal representative or other contact person. The contact information in the application is needed for sending the decision and invoice. The decision and invoice are sent by post. Report the contact person's contact information on the personal information form. [Open: personal information form.](https://www.prh.fi/material/sites/prh/forms/kaupparekisteri_fi/XeyHsmAuI/henkilotietolomake.pdf)

The application is dated, and it is signed by the person for whom the private entrepreneur permit is applied or by their authorized representative.

When an authorized person signs the application, an open power of attorney or an individualized power of attorney certified as a true copy is attached.

Note! Do not state the applicant's personal identity code or home address in the application.

### How do you submit the application?

Send the application by post to:

Patent and Registration Office  
Trade Register  
00091 PRH

or

by secure email to PRH's registry. [Read more about sending secure email.](https://www.prh.fi/en/companiesandorganisations/general-instructions-for-companies/secure-email.html)

## How much does it cost?

The application fee is 150 euros.

Additionally, an invoice fee of 6.50 euros is charged.

The fees are invoiced retroactively.

## Representative in Finland

The entrepreneur must have a representative who has the right to receive summonses and other notifications on behalf of the entrepreneur. If PRH has granted you a permit to operate as a private entrepreneur in Finland, report a representative for yourself in the Trade Register. The representative must have a permanent residence in Finland.

However, a private entrepreneur who has a residence in the EEA area does not need to appoint and report a separate representative to the register.

## What do you do with the permit?

Once you have received the permit, attach to the registration notification a copy of PRH's permit decision certified as true.
