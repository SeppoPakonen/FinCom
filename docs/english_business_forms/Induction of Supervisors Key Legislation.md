# Induction of Supervisors: Key Legislation

This document compiles the critical labor legislation for supervisor work, employer obligations, and principles of managing workplace well-being.

---

## 1. Comprehensive Supervisor Skills

The supervisor's main task is to implement the company's strategy by guiding their subordinates. Skills consist of three areas:
- **Will:** Desire to act as a supervisor, self-awareness, and desire to develop.
- **Skill:** Communication and interaction skills, emotional intelligence, and use of management tools.
- **Knowledge:** Strategic goals, understanding of role and responsibilities, and legal matters.

The supervisor acts as the employer's representative and is responsible for the unit's operations, finances, and supporting employees' professional development.

---

## 2. Managing Workplace Well-being

Workplace well-being is safe, healthy, and productive work in a well-managed organization.
- **Development targets:** Individual health, functionality of the work community, work environment, and professional skills.
- **Ethical management:** Value-based operation that commits to improving productivity and well-being simultaneously.
- **Metrics:** Sick absences, accident frequency, staff turnover, quality of development conversations, and workplace atmosphere surveys.

---

## 3. Induction and Work Guidance

Induction is the employer's responsibility. Its objective is to create a positive attitude, promote independence, and ensure occupational health and safety.
- **Content:** Organization's practices, employment contract terms, working hours, salary matters, occupational health care, safety issues, and personal responsibility areas.

---

## 4. Key Labor Legislation

### Employment Contract Act (55/2001)
Establishes the foundation for employment relationship matters.
- **Employer obligations:** Equal treatment, anti-discrimination, ensuring occupational safety, and written explanation of work conditions.
- **Probationary period:** Maximum 6 months.

### Occupational Safety and Health Act (738/2002)
Basic law of occupational safety.
- **Employer obligation:** Ensure employees' safety and health, identify hazards, and assess risks.
- **Employee obligation:** Follow instructions, use protective equipment, and report deficiencies.

### Working Hours Act (872/2019)
- **Regular working hours:** 8 h/day and 40 h/week. Possibility for flexible working hours and working time bank.
- **Rest periods:** Generally at least 11 h daily rest and 35 h weekly rest.

### Other key laws:
- **Non-Discrimination Act:** Promotes equality between women and men (equality plan mandatory if at least 30 employees).
- **Non-Discrimination Act:** Prevents discrimination (age, origin, religion, etc.).
- **Act on the Protection of Privacy in Working Life:** Processing of personal data and camera surveillance.
- **Occupational Health Care Act:** Obliges arranging services to prevent illness.
- **Annual Holidays Act:** Defines holiday length, pay, and timing.

---

## 5. Collective Agreements (TES/VES)

- **General applicability:** TES applies to unorganized employers throughout the industry as well.
- **Minimum requirements:** TES defines minimum wages and other conditions that must not be exceeded. Locally, better conditions can be agreed upon.
