# Logistics Costs

Logistics costs vary depending on the company's industry. Below is a breakdown of cost types in service industries, retail, e-commerce, and manufacturing.

## 1. Logistics costs in service industries
Even though a service company doesn't move goods, logistics still generates costs:
* **Personnel:** Salaries, benefits and training.
* **Travel:** Customer visits, travel allowances and transportation arrangements.
* **Information systems:** Customer databases, booking systems and tracking software.
* **Supplies:** Storage and management of possible materials and supplies.
* **Cancellation and return costs:** Managing customer refunds and cancellations.

## 2. Logistics costs in retail
* **Storage:** Rent, shelving space and storage equipment.
* **Procurement:** Ordering products, inbound and internal logistics.
* **Packaging:** Materials and labor for packaging.
* **Returns:** Handling returned products and restocking.

## 3. E-commerce logistics costs
* **Delivery costs:** Shipping to customers, transport fleet and fuel.
* **Customer service:** Consultation related to orders and problem solving.
* **Marketing and distribution:** Optimizing web visibility and optimizing warehouse location to improve delivery speed.

## 4. Manufacturing logistics costs
* **Procurement and SCM:** Tracking raw materials and components.
* **Production logistics:** Moving raw materials to production and finished products to storage.
* **Storage and distribution:** Storing finished products and shipping to retailers.

---

## Allocating logistics costs
Costs are taken into account in pricing and profit and loss calculations:
1. **Product-specific allocation:** Calculate the logistics share per product.
2. **Transferring costs to customer:** For example, delivery charges as a separate fee.
3. **Profitability analysis:** Evaluate how logistics affects profit margins.

---

## Artificial Intelligence (AI) in logistics
AI is utilized in logistics optimization:
* **Predictive analytics:** Assessing demand based on historical data.
* **Route optimization:** Algorithms minimize fuel consumption and delivery time.
* **Automation:** Robots and automated sorting systems in warehouses.
* **Risk management:** Predicting natural disasters or market disruptions.

### Known tools:
* **Blue Yonder:** Demand forecasting and inventory management.
* **IBM Watson Supply Chain:** Predictive analytics and visibility.
* **Oracle Logistics Cloud:** Cloud-based route optimization.
* **FourKites:** Real-time shipment tracking.
