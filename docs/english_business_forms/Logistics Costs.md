# Logistics Costs

Logistics costs form differently depending on the company's industry. Here is a breakdown of cost types in the service sector, retail, online stores, and industry.

## 1. Logistics costs in the service sector
Even though a service company does not move goods, logistics causes costs:
* **Personnel:** Salaries, additional benefits, and training.
* **Movement:** Customer visits, travel compensation, and transportation arrangements.
* **Information systems:** Customer registers, appointment systems, and monitoring software.
* **Supplies:** Storage and management of possible materials and supplies.
* **Cancellation and return costs:** Managing customer refunds and cancellations.

## 2. Logistics costs in retail
* **Storage:** Rent, shelving space, and storage equipment.
* **Procurement:** Ordering products, inbound and internal logistics.
* **Packaging:** Materials and workforce for packaging.
* **Returns:** Handling and re-stocking of products.

## 3. Logistics costs in online stores
* **Delivery costs:** Shipping to the customer, transportation fleet, and fuel.
* **Customer service:** Consulting and problem-solving related to orders.
* **Marketing and distribution:** Optimizing online visibility and optimizing warehouse location to improve delivery speed.

## 4. Logistics costs in industrial production
* **Procurement and SCM:** Tracking raw materials and components.
* **Production logistics:** Moving raw materials to production and finished products to storage.
* **Storage and distribution:** Storing finished products and shipping to resellers.

---

## Allocation of logistics costs
Costs are considered in pricing and profit calculation:
1. **Product-specific allocation:** Calculating the logistics share per product.
2. **Transferring costs to the customer:** For example, delivery costs as a separate fee.
3. **Profitability analysis:** Evaluating how logistics affects profit margin.

---

## Artificial Intelligence (AI) in logistics
AI is utilized in logistics optimization:
* **Predictive analytics:** Estimating demand with historical data.
* **Route optimization:** Algorithms minimize fuel consumption and delivery time.
* **Automation:** Robots and automatic sorting systems in warehouses.
* **Risk management:** Anticipating natural disasters or market disruptions.

### Known tools:
* **Blue Yonder:** Demand forecasting and inventory management.
* **IBM Watson Supply Chain:** Predictive analytics and visibility.
* **Oracle Logistics Cloud:** Cloud-based route optimization.
* **FourKites:** Real-time shipment tracking.
