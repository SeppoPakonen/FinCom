# PRH: Procuration (Procurators)

Procuration is a broad commercial authorization that allows the appointed person (procurator) to represent the company in all business matters.

## 1. Procurator's Powers
* **Scope:** May act on behalf of the principal and sign the trade name in business-related matters.
* **Limitations:** Without special authorization, may not transfer real estate or leasehold rights or apply for mortgages on them.
* **Legal representation:** Written procuration authorizes representing the company in courts.
* **Note:** A procurator **cannot** sign Trade Register notifications on behalf of the company.

## 2. Notification and Fee
* **Voluntary:** Reporting new procurators is voluntary but recommended.
* **Changes:** Changes to already registered information must be reported immediately.

## 3. Revocation
* Procuration can be revoked at any time by its issuer (board or partners).
* Revocation must always be reported to the Trade Register so that it is publicly known.
