# Digital Purchase Paths and Online Store Planning

This document discusses digital business operations, success factors for online stores, and strategic marketing planning from the customer's purchase path perspective.

---

## 1. Digital Business Operations and Commerce Transformation

Digital commerce covers all sales-oriented activities in a digital environment.
- **Objectives:** Increase revenue, improve profitability, and enhance efficiency.
- **Transformation:** Previously, the store was chosen, now the product is selected and purchased from the most convenient channel for the customer. Power has shifted to the customer.
- **Direct sales:** Brands and manufacturers increasingly operate directly in consumer sales (D2C), bypassing traditional retailers.

---

## 2. Success Factors for Online Stores

Successful online stores are built on three pillars:
1. **Authentic customer understanding:** Why and which customers buy from us?
2. **Customer commitment:** Meaningfulness, distinctiveness, and credibility.
3. **Strategic packaging:** Customer-oriented productization of the offering in digital format.

### Strategic Choices:
- **Channel strategy:** Factors affecting the choice include profitability, customer ownership, customer acquisition costs, and brand management.
- **Product strategy:** Range breadth, category structure, and operational capability (logistics, scalability).
- **Content strategy:** Good content is an essential competitive advantage. It builds on customer knowledge and community.

---

## 3. Establishing and Developing Online Stores

### Planning stages:
- **Goal setting:** Serving current customers vs. international scaling.
- **Platform selection:** (E.g., WooCommerce, Shopify, MyCashFlow, Vilkas, Finqu).
- **Booking systems:** (E.g., Timma, Vello, DigitalBooker).
- **Practical implementation:** Appearance, structure, payment methods, logistics, and testing on different devices.

---

## 4. Digital Purchase Path and REAN Model

Understanding the purchase path is part of quality customer service. Customer service has four roles along the purchase path: **Trust, Expertise, Service, and Sales**.

### REAN Model Along the Purchase Path:

| Phase | Goal | Actions and Metrics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **REACH** | Become found. | Content marketing, SEO/SEM, social media, influencers. (Metric: visitor count, followers). |
| **ENGAGE** | Activate and convince. | Appealing content, automatic reminders. (Metric: likes, shares, conversion). |
| **ACTIVATE** | Enable action. | Conversion optimization, clear purchase process. (Metric: number of purchases, average order). |
| **NURTURE** | Encourage return. | Post-marketing, email, social media. (Metric: newsletter subscribers, mentions). |

## 6. Social Media Publication Calendar

Planning ensures goal-oriented communication:
- **Daily:** Check channels and respond.
- **Weekly:** Publish regular content.
- **Monthly:** Larger theme, campaign, or blog post.

---

## 7. Online Store Responsibilities and Documents

Online store operators must comply with consumer protection and data protection laws:
- **Contact information:** Company name, location, and clear contact information.
- **Delivery terms:** Payment methods, delivery times, and costs.
- **Return policy:** Standard 14-day cancellation right. Customized products must be separately mentioned if they cannot be returned.
- **Product information:** Key features and total price including taxes.
- **Strong identification:** Rules according to the Payment Services Directive (PSD2).

---

## 8. Future Challenges
- **Individuality:** Moving from mass service to individual experiences.
- **Algorithms:** Differentiation and discoverability in search engines/social media becomes more difficult.
- **Simplicity:** Technology complexity must not spoil the user experience.
- **Environment:** Sustainability issues in logistics and consumption culture.
