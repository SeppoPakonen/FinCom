# ABC of Working Life: Rules and Obligations

This guide compiles key information about employment contracts, salary matters, and employee rights in Finland.

## 1. Employment Contract and Trial Period
* **Employment contract:** An agreement about work and salary. It is recommended to make it in writing.
* **Trial period:** Maximum 6 months. During the trial period, the contract can be terminated without notice period by either party (but not for discriminatory reasons).

## 2. Salary and Working Hours
* **Salary:** At least according to the industry's collective agreement (TES). A written breakdown of salary must be provided.
* **Regular working hours:** Maximum 8 h/day and 40 h/week.
* **Overtime:** Must be agreed separately, and it is compensated with an increased salary.

## 3. Annual Vacation and Leaves
* **Annual vacation:** Accumulates 2–2.5 days per month. Summer vacation (4 weeks) is taken during May–September.
* **Study leave:** Right to up to 2 years within 5 years, if the employment relationship has lasted at least one year.

## 4. Ending the Employment Relationship
* **Notice period:** Depends on the duration of the employment relationship (14 days – 6 months).
* **Immediate dismissal:** Only for very serious reasons, ends immediately.
* **Work certificate:** The employee has the right to receive a written certificate when the employment relationship ends.

## 5. Data Protection and Monitoring
* **GDPR:** The employer may only collect information that is essential for the job.
* **Monitoring:** Camera surveillance must not target individual employees or social areas. Reading emails is only permitted in exceptional cases.

---
**Remember:** The employer must ensure workplace safety and treat everyone fairly. Bullying must be addressed immediately.
