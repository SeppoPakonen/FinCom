# Template: Business Plan (BP)

You can use this outline to create a business plan for your own company. Adapt the content to your company's needs.

---

## 1. INTRODUCTION
* **1.1 Basic Company Information:** Name, contact information, company form, business sector and owners.
* **1.2 Founder Introduction:** Education, work experience and special expertise.
* **1.3 History:** How did the business idea emerge and what has been done so far?

## 2. BUSINESS CONCEPT AND IDEA
* **2.1 Business Concept:** Why does the company exist? What is its fundamental purpose?
* **2.2 Business Idea:** What is sold? To whom? How is the operation produced?
* **2.3 Vision and mission:** Where are we in 3â€“5 years? What do we want to achieve?
* **2.4 Values:** What principles guide our operations?
* **2.5 Products and services:** More detailed description of the offering and the benefit to the customer.
* **2.6 Responsibility:** How do we consider ecological and social responsibility?

## 3. BASELINE ANALYSIS
* **3.1 Customer segments:** Who are the most important customers? What are their needs?
* **3.2 Market and competitor analysis:** Market size, development trends and key competitors (strengths/weaknesses).
* **3.3 SWOT analysis:** Strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats.

## 4. MARKETING PLAN
* **4.1 Objectives:** Sales targets and desired market position.
* **4.2 Channels and methods:** Websites, social media, advertising, PR and personal sales work.
* **4.3 Annual calendar:** Timing of marketing activities.

## 5. OPERATIONAL METHOD AND RESOURCE ALLOCATION
* **5.1 Organization:** Purchasing, production, logistics and subcontractor networks.
* **5.2 Resources:** Premises, equipment, software and personnel.

## 6. FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT
* **6.1 Financial monitoring:** How is accounting and result monitoring arranged?
* **6.2 Calculations (YT4/YT5):** Investments, financing, profitability and budgets.
* **6.3 Pricing:** How are the prices of products/services formed?

## 7. RISK ASSESSMENT
* Identify the most significant risks (personnel, property, information security, liability risks) and plan measures to prepare for them (e.g. insurance).
