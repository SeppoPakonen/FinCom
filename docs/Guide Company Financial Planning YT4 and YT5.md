# Guide: Company Financial Planning (YT4 & YT5)

An important part of a business plan is financial calculations, which ensure the profitability of operations and adequacy of financing.

## 1. Calculation Programs
* **YT4:** Financial plan for a starting company (small businesses, fewer than 3 employees).
* **YT5:** Financial plan for an existing or more extensive starting company (more products, industry comparison).
* **Important:** Always use **Microsoft Excel** for calculations (Google Sheets does not properly support all formulas).

## 2. Salaries and Additional Costs (2026)
* **YEL entrepreneur:** Works in the company for > 4 months, aged 18–68.
    - Minimum earned income: 9,423.09 €/year.
    - Income qualifying for unemployment security: 15,481 €/year.
    - New entrepreneurs receive a 22% discount on fees for the first 4 years.
* **Employees (TyEL):** Additional costs for salaries are approximately 36% (includes insurance, TES supplements, sick pay).
* **Fringe benefits:** Meal allowance (lunch) approximately 150 €/month, phone allowance 20 €/month.

## 3. Operating Expenses (VAT 0%)
For budgeting purposes:
* **Premises:** In Oulu approximately 8–15 €/m², best locations 16–25 €/m². Remember security deposit (1–3 months).
* **Electricity:** approximately 0.14 €/kWh (includes transmission).
* **Marketing:** Small businesses approximately 2–5% of revenue, growth-oriented approximately 10%.
* **Bookkeeping:** Small business approximately 1,500 €/year + annual accounts 400 €.
* **Insurance:** Liability and legal protection insurance are essential. Beauty salon approximately 200 €/year, construction industry starting from 600 €/year.

## 4. Travel Expenses (2026)
* Mileage compensation: 0.55 €/km.
* Full-day allowance (> 10 h): 54 €.
* Half-day allowance (> 6 h): 25 €.

## 5. Value-Added Tax Rates (2026)
* **25.5%:** General rate (clothing, electronics, beauty salons, accounting firms, cleaning).
* **13.5%:** Food, restaurants, accommodation, books, sports, medicines.
* **10%:** Magazines, TV operations.
* **Minor operations:** If revenue is less than 20,000 €/year, there is no VAT obligation.

---
**Tip:** Budget the first year conservatively. By the third year at the latest, the entrepreneur should earn at least 10% more than as an employee in a similar job as compensation for risk.
