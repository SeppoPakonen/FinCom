# Guide: Company Financial Planning (YT4 & YT5)

An important part of the business plan is financial calculations, which ensure the profitability of operations and adequacy of financing.

## 1. Calculation Programs
* **YT4:** Financial plan for a new company (small businesses, fewer than 3 employees).
* **YT5:** Financial plan for an existing or more extensive new company (more products, industry comparison).
* **Important:** Always use **Microsoft Excel** for calculations (Google Sheets does not properly support all formulas).

## 2. Salaries and Additional Costs (2026)
* **YEL entrepreneur:** Works in the company > 4 months, 18–68 years old.
    - Minimum earned income: 9,423.09 €/year.
    - Income qualifying for unemployment benefits: 15,481 €/year.
    - New entrepreneurs receive a 22 % discount on contributions for the first 4 years.
* **Employees (TyEL):** Additional costs for salaries are approximately 36 % (including insurance, collective agreement supplements, sick pay).
* **Benefits in kind:** Meal allowance (lunch) approximately 150 €/month, phone allowance 20 €/month.

## 3. Operating Expenses (excluding VAT)
For budgeting purposes:
* **Premises:** In Oulu approximately 8–15 €/m², best locations 16–25 €/m². Remember security deposit (1–3 months).
* **Electricity:** approximately 0.14 €/kWh (including transmission).
* **Marketing:** Small businesses approximately 2–5 % of revenue, growth-oriented approximately 10 %.
* **Accounting:** Small business approximately 1,500 €/year + financial statements 400 €.
* **Insurance:** Liability and legal protection insurance are essential. Hair salon approximately 200 €/year, construction industry starting from 600 €/year.

## 4. Travel Expenses (2026)
* Mileage allowance: 0.55 €/km.
* Full day allowance (> 10 h): 54 €.
* Partial day allowance (> 6 h): 25 €.

## 5. Value-Added Tax Rates (2026)
* **25.5 %:** Standard rate (clothing, electronics, hairdressers, accounting firms, cleaning).
* **13.5 %:** Food, restaurants, accommodation, books, sports, medicines.
* **10 %:** Newspapers, TV broadcasting.
* **Minor activity:** If revenue is below 20,000 €/year, there is no VAT obligation.

---
**Tip:** Budget the first year conservatively. By the third year at the latest, the entrepreneur should earn at least 10 % more than as an employee in a comparable position as compensation for risk.
