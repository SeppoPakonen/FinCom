# Marketing Tool: AIDA Model

The AIDA model is a classical marketing tool that describes the customer's purchasing process. It can be used to plan effective advertising and sales promotion.

## 1. Model Phases

| Phase | Objective | Methods |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attention** | Capture the potential customer's attention. | Strong headlines, captivating images, distinctive colors and fonts. |
| **Interest** | Generate interest in the product or service. | Describe features and benefits. Use stories and examples. |
| **Desire** | Create desire and need to purchase the product right now. | Unique benefits, customer recommendations, limited offers. |
| **Action** | Prompt the customer to take action (Call to Action). | Clear instruction: "Buy here", "Book an appointment", "Subscribe to newsletter". |

---

## 2. Implementation in Practice

### Example: Lunch Restaurant
1. **Attention:** Social media ad with delicious dish photo: "Hungry? Today's special is waiting!"
2. **Interest:** Introduce the weekly menu highlighting ingredient freshness and chef's expertise.
3. **Desire:** "Fast service â€“ you have time to enjoy your lunch in peace." Showcase positive Google reviews.
4. **Action:** "Book a table now" or "Order takeaway and skip the queue."

### Example: Cosmetic Company
1. **Attention:** High-quality video of relaxing facial treatment: "Time for yourself?"
2. **Interest:** Tell about the benefits of the treatment (e.g. skin brightening, stress relief) and special techniques used.
3. **Desire:** Before and after photos, customer experiences. "New customers get 20% off their first visit."
4. **Action:** "Book your appointment online" or "Buy a gift card now."

---
**Tip:** Always offer an incentive (in the Action phase), such as a free sample or additional benefit, to make the threshold for action as low as possible.
