# Logistics, Competitive Advantage and Service Level

## 1. What is logistics?
Logistics is the planning, management and movement of resources with the goal of delivering goods or services to the customer efficiently.

### Key components:
* **Inventory management:** Receiving, storing and shipping. Optimal inventory level without unnecessary costs.
* **Transportation and distribution:** Road, sea, air or rail transportation.
* **Order processing:** Receiving and processing at the right time.
* **Supply Chain Management (SCM):** Cooperation between suppliers, manufacturers and distributors.
* **Information systems:** Utilizing technology (tracking, automation).
* **Quality control:** Ensuring the quality of products and services.

## 2. Logistics as a competitive advantage
Efficient logistics can provide the company with significant advantages:
* **Cost efficiency:** Savings in storage and transportation enable better margins or more competitive prices.
* **Delivery speed:** Fast deliveries are critical especially in consumer retail.
* **Customer satisfaction:** Reliability and precision bind customers.
* **Environmental friendliness:** Reducing emissions through efficient route planning attracts conscious customers.

## 3. Logistics in service industries
Even though services don't involve moving physical goods, logistics is still important:
* **Customer service management:** Optimizing time and resources (response speed, scheduling).
* **Personnel management:** Right experts in the right place at the right time.
* **Information management:** Effective use of customer profiles and order history in decision-making.

## 4. Logistics service level metrics
Metrics are used to evaluate logistics performance:

| Metric | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OTD (On-Time Delivery)** | Following the delivery schedule according to the agreed timeline. |
| **Fill rate (Order Fill Rate)** | Proportion of orders delivered completely without errors. |
| **Delivery reliability** | Ability to deliver without delays or shortages over a long period. |
| **Inventory turnover** | How quickly inventory is sold and replaced (efficiency). |
| **Delivery costs** | Monitoring transportation, storage and administrative costs. |
| **Customer satisfaction** | Feedback and evaluations of logistics services. |

## 5. Summary
Logistics optimizes resources, improves customer experience and manages risks. Regular measurement and analysis are essential for continuous improvement and maintaining competitive advantage.
