# PRH: Contributions in Kind in a Limited Liability Company

A contribution in kind means paying the subscription price for shares with assets other than cash.

## 1. Requirements for Contributions in Kind
* The asset must have **economic value for the company**.
* A commitment concerning work performance **cannot** serve as a contribution in kind.

## 2. When are Contributions in Kind Used?
* When establishing a company.
* When changing the company form.
* In paid share issues or in connection with options.
* When increasing share capital.

## 3. Required Statements and Attachments
* **Identification:** Statement in which the asset and the payment made with it are identified.
* **Valuation:** Mention of factors affecting valuation and methods used.
* **Auditor's statement:** An auditor's statement on the contribution in kind must always be attached to the notification.

## 4. Special Situations
* **Business as contribution:** Attach a decision on the matter and a statement on the financial position of the business (e.g. financial statements).
* **Real estate as contribution:** A conveyance deed must be made in the form required by the Land Register Act. Attach a sales contract or an auditor's statement on the transfer.

---
**Legislation:** Limited Liability Companies Act, Chapter 2, Section 6 and Chapter 9, Section 12.
