# PRH: Contribution in Kind in a Limited Liability Company

A contribution in kind means paying the subscription price of shares with other assets than money.

## 1. Requirements for Contribution in Kind
* The asset must have **economic value to the company**.
* A commitment concerning work performance **cannot** serve as a contribution in kind.

## 2. When is Contribution in Kind Used?
* When establishing a company.
* In changes of business form.
* In paid share issue or in connection with options.
* In increasing share capital.

## 3. Required Statements and Attachments
* **Identification:** Statement in which the asset and the payment made with it are identified.
* **Valuation:** Mention of factors affecting valuation and methods used.
* **Auditor's statement:** An auditor's statement on the contribution in kind statement must always be attached to the notification.

## 4. Special Situations
* **Business as contribution in kind:** Attach a decision on the matter and a statement on the business's financial position (e.g., financial statements).
* **Real estate as contribution in kind:** A conveyance agreement must be made in the form required by the Land Registration Act. Attach the deed of sale or an auditor's statement on the transfer.

---
**Legislation:** Limited Liability Companies Act Chapter 2 Section 6 and Chapter 9 Section 12.
