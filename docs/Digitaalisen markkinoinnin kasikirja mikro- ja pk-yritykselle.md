# Digital Marketing Handbook for Micro and Small Businesses

This document is a condensed summary of a comprehensive digital marketing guide specifically designed for domestic micro and small businesses.

---

## 1. Introduction and Digital Transformation

Digitalization has changed the rules of the market in the 2000s. Companies must adopt digital methods to remain competitive.

### Key transformation factors:
- **Democratization of information:** Power has shifted from corporations to consumers. Consumer opinions and recommendations influence purchasing decisions more than brand communication.
- **Influencers:** New opinion leaders have emerged alongside traditional media, directing attention and shaping behavior.
- **Information flood:** Vast amounts of information are available. Companies' challenge is to filter meaningful data and communicate their value proposition in seconds.
- **Reputation management:** Negative messages spread quickly. Reputation management has become a vital part of communication.

---

## 2. Unique features of digital marketing

Digital marketing differs from traditional marketing in four ways:
1. **Ubiquity:** Marketing is always present through mobile devices.
2. **Measurability:** All actions and their results can be analyzed precisely.
3. **Targetability:** Advertising can be directed based on demographics, location and behavior.
4. **Virality:** Content can spread organically through social networks, increasing its credibility.

---

## 3. SIKS Model: Foundation of Digital Marketing

Modern digital marketing is based on the SIKS model, which divides marketing into manageable entities according to the customer's purchasing process:

| Phase | Objective | Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reach** | Become known and attract visitors. | Search engine optimization (SEO), search engine marketing (SEM), brand advertising. |
| **Inspire** | Generate interest and provide information. | Content marketing, blogs, website content, interactive tools. |
| **Convert** | Guide to purchase decision and transaction. | Conversion optimization (CRO), clear purchase path, landing pages (LTO). |
| **Bind** | Create customer loyalty and advocates. | Email, CRM, customer loyalty programs, social media. |

---

## 4. Hypercompetition and strategic priorities

Dynamic industries (e.g. entertainment, technology) are characterized by hypercompetition, where competitive advantages are not permanent. Success requires three focus areas:
1. **Vision:** Ability to identify new opportunities and anticipate needs.
2. **Performance:** Ability to react quickly and act unexpectedly.
3. **Tactical expertise:** Ability to modify rules and implement changes in the organization.

---

## 5. Market Analysis

### Three M-questions of customer analysis:
- **What kind?** Who are the customers (demographics, traffic)?
- **Why?** What is the product's value to the customer (functional and intangible values)?
- **How?** Through which channels do customers arrive and how do they consume content?

### Market concepts:
- **Total market:** All potential customers.
- **Potential market:** Customers who can be reached with existing resources.
- **Reachable market:** Realistic portion of the potential market.
- **Break-even market:** Sales volume at which break-even is achieved.

---

## 6. Organization's digital capability

Digital maturity can be divided into five levels:
- **Level 1 (Baseline):** Ad hoc activities without strategy or monitoring.
- **Level 2 (Directed):** Marketing activities support other campaigns, volume-based measurement.
- **Level 3 (Defined):** Clear strategy, quality-based metrics (KPI), leadership committed.
- **Level 4 (Refined):** Business-supporting roadmap, integrated systems.
- **Level 5 (Optimized):** Continuous optimization, personalized customer experiences, ROI and customer lifetime value (CLV) based management.
