# Summary: Employment Contracts Act (55/2001)

The Employment Contracts Act is a general law that applies to the employment relationship between the employer and employee. It defines the minimum level for terms of employment.

## 1. Form and Duration of Employment Contract
* **Form:** Free form (oral, written, or electronic). However, the employer has an obligation to provide a written statement of the key terms.
* **Duration:** Indefinite duration is the general rule. Fixed-term employment always requires a justified reason (substitution, training, project work).
* **Long-term unemployed:** Can be hired on a fixed-term basis (max. 1 year) without a separate reason.

## 2. Trial Period
* At most **6 months**.
* In a fixed-term employment relationship, maximum half of the contract duration.
* During the trial period, the contract can be terminated by either party without notice period (but not for discriminatory reasons).

## 3. Employer's Obligations
* **General duty:** To promote relationships with employees and ensure work ability.
* **Equal treatment:** Prohibition of discrimination (age, health, religion, gender, etc.).
* **Sick pay:** When the employment relationship lasts > 1 month, right to full salary from the first sick day + 9 days.
* **Occupational safety:** Obligation to protect the employee from accidents and hazards.

## 4. Employee's Obligations
* **Performance of work:** Diligently and according to the employer's instructions.
* **Fidelity obligation:** Competing activity and disclosure of trade secrets are prohibited during the employment relationship.
* **Occupational safety:** Obligation to follow safety instructions and report deficiencies.

## 5. Ending the Employment Relationship
* **Dismissal:** The employer must have a justified and substantial reason.
    - Personal grounds: Serious violation of obligations, substantial deterioration of work ability.
    - Economic and production grounds: Work has substantially and permanently decreased.
* **Termination:** Only for a very substantial reason (contract ends immediately).
* **Lay-off:** Temporary suspension of work performance and salary payment (employment relationship remains in effect).
