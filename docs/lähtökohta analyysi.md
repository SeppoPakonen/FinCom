# Baseline Analyses

Baseline analyses are used to evaluate a company's business idea and operating conditions, considering both internal and external influences.

## 1. Areas of Analysis
Baseline analysis gathers information about:
* **Customers:** Target groups and purchasing behavior.
* **Markets:** Market size and potential.
* **Competitors:** Competitive situation and competitors' strengths.
* **Operating environment:** Industry development and megatrends.
* **Company and entrepreneur:** Internal resources and SWOT.

## 2. Market Analysis and Segmentation
Segmentation refers to dividing the market into smaller, homogeneous subgroups.
* **Hard criteria:** Age, gender, location, education, income level.
* **Soft criteria:** Lifestyle, values, hobbies, preferences.
* **Niche marketing:** Focusing on a small, easily reachable segment (recommended for new companies).

## 3. Competitor Analysis
Identify and analyze:
* **Core competitors:** Companies always competing directly.
* **Potential competitors:** Companies that could enter the same market.
* **Need competitors:** Satisfy the same need in different ways (e.g. theater vs. movies).

## 4. Environmental Analysis (PESTEL)
PESTEL analysis examines external factors:
* **P (Political):** Government policies, taxation, regional policy.
* **E (Economical):** Economic cycles, purchasing power, pricing.
* **S (Social):** Consumer habits, values, education level.
* **T (Technological):** Digitalization, new innovations.
* **E (Environmental):** Environmental issues, responsibility.
* **L (Legal):** Legislation and regulations.

## 5. Company Analysis (SWOT)
SWOT analysis summarizes the company's situation:

| | Positive | Negative |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Internal** | **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |
| **External** | **Opportunities** | **Threats** |

### Internal factors to assess:
* Products/services, expertise, finances, image, location, networks.

### External factors to assess:
* Demand development, industry changes, increasing competition, legislation.
