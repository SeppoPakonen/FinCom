# Logistics, Competitive Advantage and Service Level

## 1. What is logistics?
Logistics is the planning, management, and movement of resources, with the goal of delivering goods or services efficiently to the customer.

### Key components:
* **Inventory management:** Receipt, storage, and dispatch. Optimal inventory level without unnecessary costs.
* **Transportation and distribution:** Road, sea, air, or rail transport.
* **Order processing:** Receipt and processing at the right time.
* **Supply chain management (SCM):** Cooperation between suppliers, manufacturers, and distributors.
* **Information systems:** Utilization of technology (tracking, automation).
* **Quality control:** Ensuring the quality of products and services.

## 2. Logistics as a competitive advantage
Efficient logistics can provide the company with significant advantage:
* **Cost effectiveness:** Savings in storage and transportation enable better margins or more favorable prices.
* **Delivery speed:** Fast deliveries are critical especially in consumer retail.
* **Customer satisfaction:** Reliability and precision bind customers.
* **Environmental friendliness:** Reducing emissions through efficient route planning attracts conscious customers.

## 3. Logistics in the service sector
Even though services do not involve moving physical goods, logistics is still important:
* **Customer service management:** Optimization of time and resources (response speed, scheduling).
* **Personnel management:** Right specialists in the right place at the right time.
* **Information management:** Effective use of customer profiles and order history in decision-making.

## 4. Logistics service level metrics
Metrics are used to evaluate logistics performance:

| Metric | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OTD (On-Time Delivery)** | Adherence to delivery time according to the agreed schedule. |
| **Completeness rate (Order Fill Rate)** | Portion of orders delivered completely without errors. |
| **Delivery reliability** | Ability to deliver without delays or shortages over a long period. |
| **Inventory turnover rate** | How quickly inventory is sold and replaced (efficiency). |
| **Delivery costs** | Monitoring of transportation, storage, and administrative costs. |
| **Customer satisfaction** | Feedback and evaluations of logistics service. |

## 5. Summary
Logistics enables optimization of resources, improvement of customer experience, and risk management. Regular measurement and analysis are essential for continuous improvement and maintaining competitive advantage.
