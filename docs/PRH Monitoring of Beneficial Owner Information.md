# PRH: Monitoring of Beneficial Owner Information

The money laundering act obligates certain actors to monitor and report deficiencies in beneficial owner information in the Trade Register.

## 1. Obligations of Reporting Parties
Banks, auditors, and law firms must identify their customers' actual beneficial owners.
* If an operator detects a discrepancy between the information provided by the company and the Trade Register, they must make a **monitoring notification** to PRH.

## 2. Monitoring Process
1. **Monitoring notification:** An external party reports a deficiency detected to PRH.
2. **PRH letter:** PRH sends the company a request to verify and correct beneficial owner information.
3. **Correction obligation:** The company must confirm the information is correct or make a change notification.

## 3. Consequences of Negligence
If the company does not correct its information despite the request:
* PRH may impose a penalty fee.
* The company may be placed in investigation status or removed from the Trade Register.
