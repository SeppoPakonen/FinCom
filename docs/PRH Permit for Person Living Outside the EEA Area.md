# PRH: Permit for Person Living Outside the EEA Area

A person who does not have a residence in the European Economic Area (EEA) needs a permit from PRH to perform certain company management duties.

## 1. Who needs a permit?
* **Limited liability company:** Board members and managing director, if at least one member does not have a residence in the EEA area.
* **Open company and Limited partnership:** If none of the partners (general partners) have a residence in the EEA area.
* **Private entrepreneur:** If the residence is outside the EEA area.

## 2. Permit practice
* The permit is usually granted if the person permanently resides in a country participating in the Lugano Convention (e.g., **Switzerland**).
* For other countries, PRH evaluates the application on a case-by-case basis.

## 3. Applying
* **Method:** Online form at [turvaviesti.prh.fi/ilmoitus-kaupparekisteriin](https://turvaviesti.prh.fi/ilmoitus-kaupparekisteriin).
* **Attachments:** Copy of passport, statement about the company and possible translations (in Finnish or Swedish).
* **Fee:** 150 € per permit (+ 6.50 € invoice fee). Invoiced retroactively.

## 4. Representative in Finland
If the company does not have any responsible person with a residence in the EEA area, the company must have a **representative** with a domicile in Finland.

---
**Note:** Paper applications are not accepted starting from 1.1.2026.
